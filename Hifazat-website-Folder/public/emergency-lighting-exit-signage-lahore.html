Why this often gets overlooked

Many buildings in Lahore have working fire alarms and extinguishers but no emergency lighting — because it's easy to assume regular lighting is enough. It isn't. A fire is one of the most common reasons main power gets cut or fails, which is precisely the moment emergency lighting matters most. Frequently Asked Questions

Why does a building need emergency lighting if it already has regular lights?

Regular lighting fails the moment there's a power cut, which is exactly when a fire evacuation is most likely to be happening. Emergency lighting runs on battery backup, so exits, stairwells and corridors stay lit even when the main power is off — during a fire or a routine load-shedding outage.

Where should exit signage be placed in a building?

Exit signs should be visible from every point along an evacuation route — above exit doors, at corridor junctions, and on stairwell landings — so someone can find their way out without hesitation, even in smoke or low visibility.

How long does emergency lighting battery backup last?

Battery-backed emergency lights are designed to provide sufficient illumination for evacuation during a power outage. Exact backup duration depends on the unit installed — HIFAZAT recommends and installs units suited to your building's evacuation time needs.

Does emergency lighting need maintenance?

Yes. Batteries in emergency lighting units degrade over time and should be checked periodically to confirm they'll actually work during a power failure. This is included as part of HIFAZAT's Annual Maintenance Contract.
