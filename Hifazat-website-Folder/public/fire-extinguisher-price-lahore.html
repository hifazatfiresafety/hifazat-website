DCP vs CO2 — which one does your building need?

DCP (Dry Chemical Powder) is the all-rounder: it works on ordinary combustibles, flammable liquids and gas fires, which makes it the default choice for shops, offices and plaza corridors. Its one downside is powder residue — messy on electronics.

CO2 leaves no residue, which is exactly why it belongs near electrical panels, computers, server racks and sensitive machinery. It is heavier and costs more per unit, so most buildings use CO2 selectively where electronics are, and DCP everywhere else.

The practical answer for most commercial units in Lahore: DCP as the base coverage, CO2 at electrical points. Restaurants add a fire blanket at the frying station.

How many extinguishers does your building need?

Under Punjab regulations, the required count depends on floor area and building category — commercial and mixed-use buildings need denser coverage than residential buildings. Placement matters as much as count: extinguishers must be visible, mounted at reachable height, near exits and risk points, not locked in a store. A quick site inspection determines both the count and the placement plan for your specific building. Refilling — the part most owners forget

An extinguisher is not a one-time purchase. Pressure drops over time, powder cakes, and seals age — which is why annual inspection and refilling is part of compliance. During inspections, an expired extinguisher is treated as a missing one. If your building has 10+ units, an Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC) is usually cheaper and guarantees nothing silently expires.
