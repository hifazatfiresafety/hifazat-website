What's included in a HIFAZAT fire alarm system

  • Control panel — zone-based, sized to your building's floor count
  • Smoke detectors — placed per room area to give full ceiling coverage
  • Heat detectors — for kitchens, plant rooms and areas prone to false smoke alarms
  • Manual call points (MCPs) — at exits, stairwells and corridors for manual activation
  • Sounders — audible alarm coverage across every floor
  • Cabling — fire-resistant cable rated for the installation

We describe our systems accurately: these are conventional zone panels, not addressable systems. For most commercial and residential buildings in Lahore, a conventional system covers the requirement fully at a lower cost than addressable alternatives.

How installation works

  1. Site inspection. We visit your building, measure floor areas, and note kitchens, plant rooms, exits and stairwells.
  2. System design. We calculate the panel size, detector count per floor, and cable routing based on your building's actual layout.
  3. Installation. Our team installs the panel, detectors, MCPs, sounders and cabling.
  4. Testing & handover. Every zone is tested before handover, with a walkthrough of how the system works.

Which buildings need this

Commercial plazas, factories and warehouses, schools and academies, hospitals, restaurants and cafes, offices, and residential buildings all fall under fire safety requirements in Lahore. Multi-tenant commercial buildings typically need more zones and denser detector coverage than a single-family home. See our guide to LDA fire safety requirements for commercial buildings for the regulatory detail.

After installation — keeping it working

A fire alarm system needs periodic testing, not just a one-time install. Detectors can accumulate dust, batteries in MCP indicators age, and panels need functional checks. HIFAZAT's Annual Maintenance Contract (AMC) covers scheduled testing so your system stays inspection-ready year-round.

What type of fire alarm system does HIFAZAT install?

HIFAZAT installs conventional zone-based fire alarm systems — a control panel with zones matched to your building's floors, smoke detectors, heat detectors for kitchens and plant rooms, manual call points (MCPs) at exits and stairwells, and sounders for audible alarm coverage.

How many smoke detectors does my building need?

The number depends on room size and floor layout — detectors are placed to cover ceiling area without gaps. A 1200 sq ft floor typically needs more coverage than a small office. HIFAZAT calculates exact detector counts per floor during the site inspection, based on room dimensions.
