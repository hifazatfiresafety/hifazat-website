Fire Risk Assessment in Lahore

HIFAZAT Fire Safety Solutions · Updated July 2026 · Lahore, Pakistan

Most building owners don't know their exact compliance gap until something forces the question — an inspection, an incident, or a tenant asking. HIFAZAT's fire risk assessment answers it upfront: a floor-by-floor inspection of your building against LDA and Punjab Community Safety requirements, with a written report of what's covered and what's missing.

What the assessment covers

  • Existing equipment check — extinguishers, alarms, hydrants, sprinklers, wherever installed
  • Coverage gaps — floors or rooms without adequate detection or suppression
  • Exit and evacuation routes — clear paths, signage, accessibility
  • Electrical and kitchen risk points — areas needing heat detection or CO2 coverage
  • Expired or non-compliant equipment — extinguishers past service date, panels not matching current occupancy

How it works

  1. Site visit. We walk your building floor by floor, noting existing equipment and gaps.
  2. Regulation check. Findings are checked against LDA Building & Zoning Regulations 2019 and Punjab Community Safety (Buildings) Regulations 2022.
  3. Written report. You receive a clear list of what's compliant, what's missing, and what's recommended — not vague advice, specific findings.
  4. Next steps. If you want to act on the findings, we quote the equipment and installation needed to close the gaps.

Why this matters before, not after

A fire risk assessment done proactively costs nothing but time. Done reactively — after an inspection flags a violation, or worse, after an incident — it costs a lot more. For a deeper look at what LDA specifically requires by building type, see our LDA fire safety requirements guide and Rescue 1122 certificate guide.

Who this is for

New building owners preparing for occupancy, existing owners who've never had a formal assessment, plazas changing tenants, and anyone who wants to know their real compliance position before an inspector tells them. This applies across sectors — offices, schools, hospitals, restaurants, factories and residential buildings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens during a fire risk assessment?

HIFAZAT inspects your building floor by floor — checking existing extinguishers, alarm coverage, exit routes, electrical risk points and any equipment that's missing, expired or incorrectly placed. You get a written report listing exactly what's compliant and what needs attention.

Is a fire risk assessment the same as an inspection?

They overlap, but a risk assessment goes further than a basic inspection — it evaluates your building against specific LDA and Punjab Community Safety requirements and produces a documented gap report you can act on or show to authorities.

Is the initial fire risk assessment free?

HIFAZAT offers a free initial site visit to scope your building's requirements. A detailed written compliance report with specific clause references can be arranged as part of the engagement — ask on WhatsApp for current terms.

Which regulations does the assessment check against?

HIFAZAT checks buildings against LDA Building & Zoning Regulations 2019 and the Punjab Community Safety (Buildings) Regulations 2022, the two frameworks that govern fire safety compliance for buildings in Lahore.
